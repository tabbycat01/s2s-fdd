# Fault Case 1: air line blockage

## Fault class

Air line blockage. Incipient fault.

## Physical mechanism

The manual valve in the air supply line is gradually closed before the air, water and oil supply lines join at the mixing point. This simulates a developing blockage in the air line. The valve angle was recorded externally but was not included in the monitoring variable list.

## Data sets

- Set 1.1: changing operating conditions, duration 5811 s, fault from 1566 s to 5181 s.
- Set 1.2: steady-state, water 2 kg/s, air 0.0417 m3/s, duration 4467 s, fault from 657 s to 3777 s.
- Set 1.3: steady-state, water 3.5 kg/s, air 0.0208 m3/s, duration 4321 s, fault from 691 s to 3691 s.

## Expected symptoms

Likely symptoms include abnormal air delivery behavior, changes in air delivery pressure PT312, air input flow FT305, air valve VC302 response, and downstream multiphase flow consequences in riser pressure/flow variables.

## Retrieval cues

air line blockage, air supply restriction, PT312 abnormal, FT305 flow decrease, VC302 compensation, upstream air fault, incipient blockage.
