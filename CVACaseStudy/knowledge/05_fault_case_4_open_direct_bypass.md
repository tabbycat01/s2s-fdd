# Fault Case 4: open direct bypass

## Fault class

Open direct bypass. Incipient fault.

## Physical mechanism

The fault simulates a leakage or bypass at the bottom of the riser. The alternative 4 inch bypass line directs multiphase flow from after the mixing point directly to the three-phase separator, bypassing the riser and top separator. In normal operation, bypass isolation valves are closed. During this fault, they are opened gradually, reducing flow through the test area.

## Data sets

- Set 4.1: changing operating conditions, duration 7208 s, fault from 953 s to 6294 s.
- Set 4.2: steady-state, water 2 kg/s, air 0.0417 m3/s, duration 4451 s, fault from 851 s to 3851 s.
- Set 4.3: steady-state, water 3.5 kg/s, air 0.0208 m3/s, duration 3661 s, fault from 241 s to 3241 s.

## Expected symptoms

Expected evidence is a loss of flow or pressure behavior in the riser/top separator path, with abnormal riser pressure drop PT401-PT408, top riser flow FT407, top separator output FT406, and separator level/pressure behavior. The physical meaning is not a restriction inside the riser but a diversion of flow away from it.

## Retrieval cues

open bypass, direct bypass, riser leakage, lack of flow in test area, flow diverted to three-phase separator, PT401-PT408, FT407 decrease, FT406 abnormal.
