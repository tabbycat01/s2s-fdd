# Fault Case 6: pressurization of the 2 inch line

## Fault class

Pressurization of the 2 inch line. Abrupt abnormal operation.

## Physical mechanism

In normal benchmark operation, the 4 inch line carries flow and the 2 inch line is isolated. At the top of the riser, a bridge valve can connect the 4 inch and 2 inch lines. Fault Case 6 opens this bridge valve while keeping both sides of the 2 inch line isolated, causing the isolated 2 inch line to become pressurized.

The paper describes this as an unusual operation that could degrade performance, affect product quality, or create safety issues. It should not strongly alter flow conditions in the 4 inch line, but it should pressurize the 2 inch line.

## Data sets

- Set 6.1: changing operating conditions, duration 2800 s, fault from 1723 s to 2800 s.
- Set 6.2: changing operating conditions, duration 4830 s, fault from 1037 s to 4830 s.

## Expected symptoms

Variable x24, PT417 pressure in the mixture zone of the 2 inch line, is the key direct measurement and is only included for this case. Because the fault is abrupt, detection can be fast. If x24 is unavailable, the fault may be hard to separate from other operating changes because the 4 inch line is not expected to be strongly affected.

## Retrieval cues

2 inch line pressurization, PT417, x24, isolated line pressure, bridge valve open, abrupt fault, abnormal operation, safety issue.
